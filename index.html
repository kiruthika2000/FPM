How I work

Three rules for payment product management.

Fraud prevention is a product, not a filter

I don't bolt fraud detection on at the end. I design it into the payment flow from day one — 14 risk signals evaluated in real time before a wire is approved. 93% accuracy means real fraud gets caught. But equally important: legitimate payments don't get blocked. False positives are product failures too.

Regulation E is a feature spec

The 10-business-day provisional credit timeline isn't a legal annoyance — it's a product requirement. I translate regulatory mandates into engineering tickets. When the dispute workflow takes 8 days and the law says 10, I don't celebrate. I automate it to 18 hours and make the customer feel protected, not processed.

Value = revenue + CX − fraud risk

Every roadmap decision is a three-variable equation. More aggressive fraud screening reduces losses but also blocks legitimate transfers. I use value-based analysis to find the optimal point — and I present that trade-off to leadership with strategic storytelling, not just spreadsheets.
